Three Hygiene Checks Before You Sign

The video's three rules — validated with real numbers. Fail any one and you're overextending.

1

Breathability Test

All EMIs combined ≤ 30% of monthly take-home. Below 25% = peace of mind. Above 40% = job loss → crisis.

₹1.2Cr loan @ 8%, 20yr → EMI ₹1,00,374/mo
Need take-home ≥ ₹3.35L/mo to stay at 30%
2

Value Filter

Home price ≤ 5× your annual income. Simple ceiling. Prevents lifestyle inflation from pushing you into a home you can't afford.

Annual income ₹30L → max home ₹1.5Cr
Annual income ₹20L → max home ₹1.0Cr
Annual income ₹12L → max home ₹60L
3

Sleep-Well Fund

Never drain all savings for down payment. Keep 6–12 months of EMI as liquid emergency reserve before buying.

EMI ₹1L/mo → keep ₹6–12L liquid
Do NOT liquidate FDs, gold, or mutual funds to make down payment

Where the "Profit" Actually Goes
The Interest Illusion: People say "my house went from ₹1.5Cr to ₹3Cr." But they paid ₹54.7L in interest — that's money that already left their hands via EMIs and was never counted. The true cost of the house was ₹2.05Cr, not ₹1.5Cr. On that real cost, the CAGR is just 3.1% — worse than a fixed deposit.

So When Should You Buy?

The video's conclusion isn't "don't buy" — it's "buy only when you understand the full cost and pass all three checks."

The bottom line from the video (data-confirmed): A house is a lifestyle asset first, investment second. The real ROI after interest and taxes is 3–5% CAGR — comparable to an FD, not equity. Buy when it makes life sense, not spreadsheet sense. Know the cost. Pass the three checks. Don't over-extend.
