Data-Validated Takeaways
📉 CTC is fiction
A 1Cr CTC can have the same in-hand as a 40L CTC. Strip out variables and one-time payments before comparing offers.
🧱 Fixed costs are a cage
Player 2's 71% fixed commitment eliminates financial optionality. Job loss = immediate crisis. Keep fixed costs below 50%.
🎯 Allocation > Amount
Nikil saves 2.2× more than Harish but could end up with less. Where money goes matters more than how much goes in.
🌀 Diversification is boring & correct
20 years of Indian market data show no single asset wins every decade. Equity led 6/11 rolling decades, Gold led 5. You can't predict which.
⚡ Savings rate > Salary
46% savings rate on ₹95k beats 16% on ₹2.49L. The gap compounds massively over 20 years.
🧭 Define your own finish line
The video's final message: wealth without a personal definition of enough is a treadmill. Journal. Figure out what you actually want. It costs less than you think.

Who's Actually Winning?

By pure numbers, Harish — the "least successful" player — has the best risk-adjusted wealth trajectory. Player 2 earns the most predictably but is consuming it all. Nikil has the highest ceiling and the lowest floor.

The real winner is whoever first stops optimising their CTC and starts optimising their savings rate, allocation, and cost structure.
